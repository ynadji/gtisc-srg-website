GTISC Security Reading Group
Spring 2010

Overview

The GTISC Security Reading Group is a weekly informal seminar for discussing research papers, emerging problems, and interesting issues in computer and network security. This semester the SRG is organized by Roberto Perdisci and run by all of the students in the GTISC lab. Lunches are paid for by GTISC (thanks!). If you are interested in participating, please read the "Informaion for Participants" section below. If you have any questions please contact Roberto at < perdisci AT gtisc DOT gatech DOT edu >

Information for Participants

Papers should be recent (typically not older than one year) and should be chosen from one of the following conferences: IEEE S&P (Oakland), Usenix Security, NDSS, CCS, SOSP, OSDI, SIGCOMM, or NSDI. Papers from other conferences (e.g, RAID, Esorics, ACSAC, DSN, IMC) are acceptable if the paper is particularly good. We will read one paper per week. One person will be responsible to choose and present the paper, and should be able to provide some background information on the related work and research trends in the area subject of discussion. It should go without saying, but since this is a reading group it is expected that everyone reads the paper before coming to the discussion!
